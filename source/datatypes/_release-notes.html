Pharmacy Work Group Changes

  • Dosage.simple removed (FHIR-54651)
    A simple dosage is now expressed as a single step. The simple element has been removed as it was redundant. If only one step is present, DosageDetails.step.safety shall not be populated.
  • Entered-in-error status definitions corrected (FHIR-54903)
    The entered-in-error status for MedicationStatement and MedicationRequest now simply indicates that the resource was entered in error and is not valid.
  • MedicationStatement.reason vs .relatedClinicalInformation clarified (FHIR-54451)
    It is now clarified that .reason covers what leads to the medication being taken (or not taken), and .relatedClinicalInformation is for information relevant to the statement but not the actual indication for the medication — that belongs in .reason.
  • Dosage.site is now CodeableReference(BodyStructure) (FHIR-54848)
    Dosage.site can now reference a BodyStructure for richer site descriptions including laterality and location qualifiers. The same coded binding is retained.
  • DosageDetails.renderedInstruction is now required (1..1) (FHIR-54014)
    renderedInstruction must always be present to ensure the full dosage intent is communicated in every case.
  • Dosage examples corrected: step.component (FHIR-56016)
    Dosage examples now correctly place timing and doseAndRate under step.component rather than directly under step.
  • Dosage examples: structural representation corrected to match text (FHIR-54523)
    Fixed mismatches between narrative and structure (e.g. Example 1 doseQuantity was 10mg instead of 2.5mg) and cross-format inconsistencies in Examples 2 and 3 (missing dose, wrong units, wrong safety period).
